What is low carbon life


Low-carbon means lower (lower) emissions of greenhouse gases (mainly carbon dioxide). Low-carbon life can be understood as: reducing carbon dioxide emissions, low-energy, low-consumption, and low-cost lifestyles. Today, this trend is gradually rising in some big cities in our country, subtly changing people's lives. Low-carbon life represents healthier, more natural, safer, return to basics to carry out human and nature activities. In today's society, with the development of human life and the improvement of living material conditions, it has also brought influence and changes to the surrounding environment of human beings. For ordinary people, it is an attitude towards life. Low-carbon life is not only a way of life, but also an environmental responsibility for sustainable development.

Nowadays, the low-carbon way of life has quietly entered many countries, and many low-carbon websites have begun to popularize an interesting special calculator for calculating personal carbon emissions, such as the low-carbon calculator of Urban Low-Carbon Economic Network, which is vivid and interesting. The animation form can not only calculate the carbon emissions of daily life, but also show the impact of different lifestyles, housing structures and new technologies on carbon emissions, and reduce driving.

Low-carbon is a life attitude for ordinary people, and it has also become a new way for people to promote the trend. It asks us a question of "whether you are willing to create a low-carbon life with everyone", but we should actively promote it. And to practice low-carbon life, we must pay attention to saving electricity, solar terms, and turning off lights for one hour...start from these little things. In addition to planting trees, there are also people who buy products with short transportation distances, and some people insist on climbing stairs. But the premise is to do our best to save energy and reduce emissions without reducing the quality of life.

"Energy saving and emission reduction" is not only a buzzword in today's society, but also a strategic choice related to the future of mankind. It is of great significance to raise the awareness of "energy conservation and emission reduction", make simple and feasible changes to one's own lifestyle or consumption habits, and together reduce global greenhouse gas (mainly carbon dioxide) emissions. "Low-carbon life" saves energy and protects the environment, which is conducive to slowing down the speed of global warming and environmental degradation. Reducing carbon dioxide emissions and choosing a "low-carbon life" are the responsibilities and obligations of every citizen. Low-carbon is to advocate a low-energy, low-consumption, and low-expenditure lifestyle to minimize energy consumption, thereby reducing carbon dioxide emissions, protecting the global environment, and ensuring long-term comfortable and comfortable life and development for human beings on the earth. Low-carbon life is an economical, healthy and happy way of life, it will not reduce people's happiness index, on the contrary it will make our life happier.


What is the advantage of low carbon life?


(1) Benefits to the natural environment

Low-carbon life is of great value in solving the moral relationship between human beings and the natural environment. Therefore, each of us has the responsibility to actively participate in creative activities and publicity work on low-carbon life from all aspects of daily life. In the tide of building a resource-saving and environment-friendly society, low-carbon life should become the practical action of the whole people.

(2) Benefits to economic development

At the same time, low-carbon development is also a rare opportunity for a country's sustainable development and transformation of economic development mode. Therefore, it is our responsibility and mission to advocate a low-carbon economy and improve lifestyles, which is conducive to promoting the construction of ecological civilization, promoting sound and rapid economic and social development, and improving the quality of life of the people.

(3) Promote the harmonious coexistence between man and nature

As the core content of environmental awareness, the social value concept based on the mutual benefit relationship between human beings and nature determines people's environmental behavior orientation. Facing the reality of such serious environmental problems, people should also understand the relationship between human beings and the natural environment, understand that the natural environment meets human needs, and how human beings should understand environmental problems and how to protect the environment should have a deep understanding.

How can we take steps to low carbon life

A set of "low carbon figures", you know?

★If a computer is used for 4 hours a day and turned off at other times, it can save about 500 yuan per year and reduce carbon emissions by 83%.
★If a person drinks less alcohol by 0.5kg a year, he can reduce carbon emissions by 1kg.
★One million plastic bags are used every minute in the world. Using less than one plastic bag can reduce carbon emissions by 0.1 gram; using less than 10% of disposable chopsticks can reduce carbon emissions by 103,000 tons per year.
★The water-saving shower head can not only save 10 liters of water, but also reduce the carbon emissions caused by a 3-minute hot shower by half.
★Using 11W energy-saving lamps instead of 60W incandescent lamps and lighting for 4 hours a day, one energy-saving lamp can reduce carbon dioxide emissions by 68.6 kg in one year; turning off the lights can reduce carbon dioxide emissions by 4.7 kg.
★If you drive less than one day per month, the annual carbon emissions per car can be reduced by 98 kg. In addition, a car with a displacement of 1.3 liters can reduce emissions by 647 kg per year. By replacing the air filter in time, maintaining proper tire pressure, and turning off the engine in time, the emission of each vehicle can be reduced by 400 kg per year.

Which lifestyles around us are low-carbon?

①Use less paper towels and pick up handkerchiefs again; ②Every paper is printed on both sides; ③Do not take elevators and stairs, and clean the air conditioner regularly, which is healthy and saves electricity; ④Grow flowers and plants at home to absorb the carbon dioxide emitted by yourself; Do it during the day; ⑥It is not because of laziness to wash clothes in a bucket, but to save water and electricity; ⑦Having short meetings is also a way to save lighting, air conditioning, audio, etc.; It consumes a lot of electricity; ⑨If it is not necessary, try to buy local and seasonal products, transportation and packaging are often more energy-intensive than production; ⑩Try to watch TV less, choose to dry clothes, avoid using tumble dryers, lunch breaks and after work Turn off the computer power supply, and try to ride a bicycle or walk to and from work.

What are some low-carbon tips in daily life?

①Pave the bottom of the wardrobe with waste newspapers, which can not only absorb moisture, but also absorb the peculiar smell in the wardrobe; it can also scrub the glass to reduce the use of glass cleaners that pollute the environment; ②Use the used mask paper to wipe jewelry, Wipe the surface of the furniture or wipe the belt, not only to polish it but also to leave the aroma of the mask paper; ③Using dried tea dregs as a pillow is not only comfortable, but also helps to improve sleep; it can also be used to absorb odors;④ When going out shopping, try to bring your own reusable bags; ⑤ Bring your own drinking cups when you go out, and reduce the use of disposable cups; ⑥ Use more permanent chopsticks and lunch boxes, try to bring your own tableware, and avoid using disposable tableware; First open the windows to let the indoor air change naturally, then turn on the electric fan to cool down the room first, then turn on the air conditioner and adjust the room temperature to between 25 and 26°; ⑧ Wash the used plastic bottles and use them to hold various liquid substances; ⑨Use food waste and residues as fertilizers; ⑩Use rice washing water to wash hands, face, dishes, furniture, and flowers.
